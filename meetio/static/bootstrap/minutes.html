Daily SCRUM

Scrum meetings are a special kind of stand-up meeting in which everyone tells about their progress of the day before and their goals for that day. Features that need to be implemented into a release are so called user stories and bundled into sprints. The amount of time left to complete a sprint is estimated and plotted daily. From this burndown chart a burndown velocity can be calculated. This tells you when the release will be ready and if you are on track or behind.

The scrum master keeps track on the burndown chart and organises this meeting daily. A meeting should be timeboxed to 15 minutes.
